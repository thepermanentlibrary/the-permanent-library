1. Nature of This Service

The Permanent Library is an open-source software tool that provides two functions:

A. Uploader: A client-side web interface that encodes documents into blockchain-compatible calldata and facilitates their submission as transactions from the user's own cryptocurrency wallet to a provably unowned burn address (0x734F6C30fcd31819c46E49B98C69D89978446fa6) on supported public blockchains (Ethereum, Polygon, and Arbitrum).

B. Indexer and Search Portal: A read-only service that scans publicly available blockchain data for transactions matching the PERMLIB-V1 encoding specification, and presents them in a searchable format.

The Permanent Library does not store, host, or transmit user-uploaded content. All documents are written directly to public blockchains by the user's own wallet. The Permanent Library's servers never receive, process, or relay document content at any point during the upload process. The indexer only reads data that is already publicly and permanently available on the blockchain.

2. The Permanent Library Is a Tool, Not a Publisher

The Permanent Library is a software tool, not a content platform, publisher, or hosting service. It functions analogously to a blockchain explorer (such as Etherscan) — it reads and displays data that already exists on public blockchains.

The operators of The Permanent Library:

  • Do not select, curate, edit, approve, endorse, or modify any content uploaded by users.
  • Do not have the ability to remove, alter, or censor any content once it has been written to the blockchain by a user. This is an inherent and intentional property of blockchain technology.
  • Do not have access to, custody of, or control over any user's cryptocurrency wallet, private keys, or funds.
  • Do not operate or control the blockchain networks on which data is stored.
  • Have no knowledge of what content a user intends to upload before the user independently signs and broadcasts a blockchain transaction from their own wallet.
